How it began...


The rubber duck first emerged from rubber manufacturing in the late 19th century. The earliest rubber ducks were made from vulcanized rubber as that was what they had to work with at the time. As such, they could not float like the ones today.

In the 1940's, Sculptor Peter Ganine created a sculpture of a duck. It was then patented and reproduced as a floating toy. As it is now, currently over 50 million have been sold.

Where we are now!


Rubber ducks are now collected by enthusiasts the world over. The 2011 Guinness World Record for World's Largest Rubber Duck Collection had 5,631 different rubber ducks. In 2013, the rubber duck was even inducted into the Toy Hall of Fame in a museum in Rochester, New York, along with the game of chess.
